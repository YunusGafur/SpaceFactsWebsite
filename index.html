Our Solar System

Mercury

Size: 1,516 mi

Distance from the sun: 36.04 million miles

Fun Facts: Despite being close to the sun, Mercury can still have ice in permanently shadowed craters due to its extreme temperature fluctuations.

Venus

Size: 7,521 miles

Distance from the sun: 67 million miles

Fun Facts: A day on Venus is longer than a year on Venus, meaning it takes longer to rotate once on its axis than to orbit the sun.

Earth

Size: 7,917 miles

Distance from the sun: 93 million miles

Fun Facts: Though not perfectly round, Earth is slightly bulging at the equator due to its rotation, making it an oblate spheroid.

Mars

Size: 4,222 miles

Distance from the sun: 142 million miles

Fun Facts: Often called the "Red Planet" because of the iron oxide dust covering its surface, making it appear rusty.

Jupiter

Size: 88,846 miles

Distance from the sun: 484 million miles

Fun Facts: As the largest planet in our solar system, Jupiter has the shortest day, taking only about 10 hours to complete one rotation.

Saturn

Size: 74,698 miles

Distance from the sun: 889 million miles

Fun Facts: While the exact age is unknown, Saturn's iconic rings are thought to be relatively young and could be made up of broken-up moons.

Uranus

Size: 31,763 miles

Distance from the sun: 1.8 billion miles

Fun Facts: Unlike other planets, Uranus spins almost completely on its side, likely due to a past collision.

Neptune

Size: 30,775 miles

Distance from the sun: 2.8 billion miles

Fun Facts: Neptune has the strongest winds in the solar system, with gusts exceeding 1,200 mph.
